Chicken Cacciatore

Description

Chicken Cacciatore is a classic Italian dish that translates to "hunter's chicken." It's a flavorful stew made with chicken, tomatoes, onions, bell peppers, mushrooms, and herbs. Here's a basic recipe for you:

Chicken cacciatore, or "pollo alla cacciatora" in Italian, has its roots in rustic Italian cuisine. The name "cacciatore" means "hunter" in Italian, reflecting the dish's origin as a hearty, flavorful stew that hunters would prepare using readily available ingredients. Traditionally, it was made with whatever game was on hand, such as rabbit or chicken, and simmered with vegetables, tomatoes, and herbs. The dish embodies the simplicity and resourcefulness of Italian home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken thighs or legs (bone-in, skin-on)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup dry white wine (optional, can substitute with chicken broth)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Brown the Chicken:
    • Heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
    • Season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper.
    • Add the chicken to the skillet and cook for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until browned. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  2. Cook the Vegetables:
    • In the same skillet, add the onion and bell pepper. Cook for about 5 minutes, or until softened.
    • Add the garlic and mushrooms, and cook for another 3 minutes.
  3. Deglaze the Pan:
    • Pour in the white wine (or chicken broth) and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly.
  4. Add Tomatoes and Seasonings:
    • Stir in the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes. Mix well.
  5. Simmer:
    • Return the chicken to the skillet, nestling it into the tomato mixture.
    • Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook for 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  6. Garnish and Serve::
    • Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Chicken cacciatore goes great with pasta, rice, or crusty bread. Enjoy your meal!
