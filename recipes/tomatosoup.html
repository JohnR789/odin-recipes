Tomato Soup

Description

Tomato Soup is a Creamy, rich tomato soup with fresh basil.

Tomato Soup, became popular in the 19th century, especially in the U.S., where Campbell's introduced its canned version in 1897, making it a classic comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 800g canned tomatoes (or 6-7 ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped)
  • 500ml vegetable stock (or chicken stock)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato purée
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • Fresh basil leaves (a handful)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Crème fraîche or heavy cream (optional for garnish)

Steps

  1. Sauté the vegetables:
    • Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onions, garlic, and carrot. Sauté for 5-6 minutes until soft and translucent.
  2. Add tomatoes and stock:
    • Stir in the canned tomatoes, tomato purée, and sugar. Pour in the vegetable stock. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Simmer:
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Blend:
    • Using a hand blender, blend the soup until smooth (or transfer to a blender in batches). If you prefer a chunkier texture, leave a few tomato pieces intact.
  5. Finishing touches:
    • Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as necessary. Stir in some fresh basil leaves for a fragrant finish.
  6. Garnish and Serve:
    • Ladle the soup into bowls and drizzle with a little crème fraîche or cream if desired. Garnish with additional basil leaves.

Tomato Soup is best served hot. Serve with warm crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich for a cozy meal on a fall day!
